Central Park- formerly known as Seneca Village- is a well-known park that divides The Upper West and The Upper East Side. Despite it's horrible backstory, it's one of New York City's most visited attraction filled with various activies and beautiful views. Popular attractions include: horse and carriage rides, waks and runs, Central Park Zoo, hundreds of statutes and monuments, picnic areas and so much more!

The Met

The Metropolitan Meseum of Art (The Met Museum) is one of the most famous museums in the world. Due to it's diverse collections of art, The Met has made itself undeniably one of the New York City's best locations to visit with over two million works divided among seventeen departments. The met museum has brought in art from all over the world. You can find this cathedral of art on Fifth Avenue along Central Park on the Upper East Side.

Empire State Building

The Empire State Building is an Art Deco skyscraper in Midtown Manhattan in New York City. Even though the building is mainly used for office space, it's a great tourist attraction surrounded by shops and eateries on the ground floors, as well as observation decks you can visit on the 80th, 86th and 102nd floors.
